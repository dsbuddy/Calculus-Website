PRACTICE PROBLEMS:

  1. Consider the function \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\).

    1. Find the local linear approximation \(p_1(x)\) and the local quadratic approximation \(p_2(x)\) to \(f(x)\) at \(x=4\).



      1 \(p_1(x)=2+\frac{1}{4}(x-4)\)

      \(p_2(x)=2+\frac{1}{4}(x-4)-\frac{1}{64}(x-4)^2\)

      Note that \(p_1(x)\) and \(p_2(x)\) are just the 1st and 2nd Taylor polynomials for \(f(x)\) about \(x=4\).

    2. Approximate \(\sqrt{4.1}\) using your answers in part (a).



      1 \(p_1(4.1)=2+\frac{1}{4}(4.1-4)=\frac{81}{40}=2.025\)

      \(p_2(4.1)=2+\frac{1}{4}(4.1-4)-\frac{1}{64}(4.1-4)^2=\frac{81}{40}-\frac{1}{6400}=2.02484375\)

      Calculator: \(\sqrt{4.1} \approx 2.024845673\).
