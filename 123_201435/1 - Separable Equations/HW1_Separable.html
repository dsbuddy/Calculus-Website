PRACTICE PROBLEMS:

  1. Verify that \(y=x^2+1\) is a solution to the differential equation \(y-\frac{dy}{dx}=(x-1)^2\).

    1Differentiating \(y=x^2+1\) with respect to \(x\) yields \(y^{\prime}=2x\). Thus,
    \[y-\frac{dy}{dx}=(x^2+1)-(2x)=(x-1)^2\]

  2. Find the value(s) of the constant \(A\) for which \(y=e^{Ax}\) is a solution to the differential equation \(y^{\prime\prime}+5y^{\prime}-6y=0\).

    \(A=-6\) and \(A=1\)
