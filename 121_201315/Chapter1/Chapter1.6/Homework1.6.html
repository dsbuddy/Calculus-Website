For problem 24-28, determine the value(s) of \(x\) where the given function is continuous.

  1. \(f(x) = \csc{x}\)

    \(f(x)\) is continuous for all \(x \neq \pi k\), where \(k\) is any integer.

  2. \(f(x) = e^{\sin {x}}\)

    \(f(x)\) is always continuous.

  3. \(\displaystyle f(x) = \frac{1}{1-2\cos{x}}\) on \([0,2\pi]\)

    \(f(x)\) is continuous for all \(x\) in \([0,2\pi]\) except for \(\displaystyle x=\frac{\pi}{3}\) and \(\displaystyle x=\frac{5\pi}{3}\)

  4. \(f(x) = \sin^{-1}{x}\)

    \(f(x)\) is always continuous.

  6. Find all non-zero value(s) of \(k\) so that \(\displaystyle f(x)=\left\{\begin{array}{lll} \frac{3\sin{(kx)}}{x} & \text{if} & x>0\\ &&\\ 6k^2+5x & \text{if} & x \leq 0 \end{array}\right.\) is continuous at \(x=0\).

    \(\displaystyle k=\frac{1}{2}\)

  7. Use the Intermediate Value Theorem to prove that there is at least one solution to \(\cos{x}=x^2\) in \((0,1)\).

    1Let \(f(x)=\cos{(x)}-x^2\). Since \(f(x)\) is continuous on \((-\infty,\infty)\), it is also continuous on \([0,1]\). Notice that \(f(0)=1>0\) and \(f(1)=\cos{(1)}-1<0\). Thus, the Intermediate Value Theorem states that there must be some \(c\) in \((0,1)\) such that \(f(c)=0\). i.e., there must be at least one \(c\) in \((0,1)\) such that \(\cos(c)-c^2=0\) \(\implies\) \(\cos{(c)}=c^2\), as desired.
