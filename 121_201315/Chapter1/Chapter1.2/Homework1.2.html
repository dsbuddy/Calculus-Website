EXPECTED SKILLS:

  • Know the basic properties of limits; i.e., be familiar with how limits “interact” with sums, differences, products, and other operations. See Theorem 1.2.2.

  • Given the formula of a function \(y=f(x)\), be able to determine the limit of \(f(x)\) as \(x\) approaches some finite value (as both a one-sided and two sided limit).

  • Be able to determine when such a limit does not exist, and if appropiate, indicate if the behavior of the function is increasing or decreasing without bound.

  • Be familiar with the indeterminate forms of \(\displaystyle \frac{0}{0}\) and \(\displaystyle \frac{\pm \infty}{\pm \infty}\). And, know how to use algebraic techniques such as factoring and rationalizing to help compute these types of limits.

PRACTICE PROBLEMS:

In each problem, compute the limit. If the limit doesn’t exist write \(+\infty\), \(-\infty\), or DNE (whichever is most appropriate).

  1. Given that \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 1}{f(x)} = 4\) and \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 1}{g(x)}=2 \), determine each of the following limits:

    1. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 1}{\left(f(x)+g(x)\right)}\)

    2. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 1}{\left(5f(x)-g(x)\right)}\)

    3. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 1}{\left(\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right)}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 1}{\left(x^2+1\right)}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 4}{1}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow -1}{(x+1)(x^3)}\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 5^-}{\left(\frac{x^2-6x}{x^3-1}\right)}\)

  6. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow -1}{\left(\frac{x^2-1}{x+1}\right)}\)

  7. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 2^-}{\left(\frac{x^2-4x+4}{x-2}\right)}\)

  8. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 3^+}{\left(\frac{x^2+2x-15}{x-3}\right)}\)

  9. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow 1}{\left(\frac{x^3-3x^2-x+3}{x^2-1}\right)}\)

  10. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 16}{\left(\frac{\sqrt{x}-4}{x-16}\right)}\)

  11. \(\lim_{x \rightarrow 0}\left(\frac{|x|}{x}\right)\)

  12. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 4^-}{\left(\frac {x}{x-4}\right)}\)

  13. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 4^+}{\left(\frac{x}{x-4}\right)}\)

  14. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 4}{\left(\frac{x}{x-4}\right)}\)

  15. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow -2}{\left(\frac{1}{x-2}\right)}\)

  16. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow -2^-}{\left(\frac{x}{x^2+2x}\right)}\)

  17. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow -2^+}{\left(\frac{x}{x^2+2x}\right)}\)

  18. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 3}{\left(\frac{x^3}{|x-3|}\right)}\)

  19. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow 1^-}{\left(\frac{x-1}{x^2-2x+1}\right)}\)

  20. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow 1^+}{\left(\frac{x-1}{x^2+2x-3}\right)}\)

  21. Let \(\displaystyle f(n) = \begin{cases} n^2+1, & \text{if }n \leq -1 \\ 3n+1, & \text{if }n > -1 \end{cases}\). Compute \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\rightarrow -1}{f(n)}\)

  22. Let \(\displaystyle f(x) = \begin{cases} 3x^3+2x-3, & \text{if }x < 1 \\ 100, & \text{if }x = 1 \\ \frac{x^2-1}{x-1}, & \text{if }x > 1 \end{cases}\). Determine \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 1}{f(x)}\)

  23. Let \(\displaystyle f(p) = \begin{cases} 3p-1, & \text{if } p<3 \\ p^3-4p-7, & \text{if } p>3 \end{cases}\). Find \(\displaystyle \lim_{p\rightarrow 3}{f(p)}\)

  24. Let \(f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2+2ax+a^2, & \text{if } x < 4 \\ 432, & \text{if } x = 4 \\ x^2 -7, & \text{if } x > 4 \end{cases}\). Find the value(s) of \(a\) such that \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow 4}{f(x)}\) exists.

  25. Let \(x_0\) be a fixed real number. Compute \(\displaystyle \lim_{h \rightarrow 0}{\frac{(x_0+h)^2-x_0^2}{h}}\)

  26. Let \(x_0\) be a fixed real number. Compute \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \rightarrow x_0}{\frac{x^2-x_0^2}{x-x_0}}\)

  27. Let \(x_0\) be a fixed, positive real number. Compute \(\displaystyle \lim_{h \rightarrow 0}{\frac{\sqrt{x_0+h}-\sqrt{x_0}}{h}}\)
