  10. Let \(f(x)=x^2\).

    1. Calculate the Local Linear Approximation, \(L(x)\), for \(f(x)\) at \(x=a\).



      \(L(x)=2ax-a^2\)

    2. Does \(L(x)\) overestimate or underestimate \(f(x)\) near \(x=a\)? Explain.



      1

      \(L(x)\) is always an underestimate of \(f(x)\) for \(x\) near \(a\). Graphically, this should make sense since the tangent line to \(f(x)=x^2\) is always below its graph.

      To show that \(L(x)\) is always an underestimate, we will show that \(L(x)\leq f(x)\) for all values of \(x\). In other words, we will show that \(L(x)-f(x) \leq0\) for all \(x\). So, we compute:

      \[\begin{aligned} L(x)-f(x) &= 2ax-a^2-x^2\\ &=-(x^2-2ax+a^2)\\ &=-(x-a)^2\\ & \leq0\end{aligned}\]
