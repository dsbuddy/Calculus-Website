Review of Log Properties
For all of these problems, assume that \(b>0\), \(b \neq 1\), \(M>0\), and \(N>0\).

  1. Suppose you have the logarithmic equation \(y=\log_b{x}\). What is an equivalent exponential equation? i.e., What is an equivalent statement in terms of inverse functions?

  2. Suppose that you have the quantities \(x=\log_b{M}\) and \(y=\log_b{N}\).

    1. Rewrite each of these logarithmic equations as equivalent exponential equations.



      \(b^x=M\) and \(b^y=N\)

    2. Using part (a), compute \(MN\) and simplify completely.



      \(MN=b^xb^y=b^{x+y}\)

    3. Now take \(log_b\) of both sides of your answer from part (b) and simplify.



      \(\log_b{(MN)}=\log_b{(b^{x+y})}=x+y\)

    4. Replace \(x\) and \(y\) with their definitions (from above) and recognize that you have just proven the following rule of logarithms: \(\log_b{(MN)}=\log_b{M}+\log_b{N}\).
