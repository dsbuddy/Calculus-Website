SUGGESTED REFERENCE MATERIAL:

As you work through the problems listed below, you should reference Chapter 11.6 of the recommended textbook (or the equivalent chapter in your alternative textbook/online resource) and your lecture notes.

EXPECTED SKILLS:

  • Be able to find the equation of a plane that satisfies certain conditions by finding a point on the plane and a vector normal to the plane.

  • Know how to find the parametric equations of the line of intersection of two (non-parallel) planes.

  • Be able to find the (acute) angle of intersection between two planes.

For problems 2-6, determine whether the following are parallel, perpendicular, or neither.

  1. Plane \(P_1:5x-3y+4z=-1\) and plane \(P_2: 2x-2y-4z=9\)

  2. Plane \(P_1: 3x-2y+z=-3\) and plane \(P_2: 5x+y-6z=8\)

For problems 8-13, find an equation of the plane which satisfies the given conditions.

  1. The plane which passes through the point \(P(1,2,3)\) and which has a normal vector of \({\bf n}=4{\bf i}-2{\bf j}+6{\bf k}\).

  2. The plane which passes through \(P(-2,0,1)\) and is perpendicular to the line \(\overrightarrow{\ell}(t)=\langle 1,2,3 \rangle +t \langle 3,-2,2\rangle\).

  3. The plane which passes through points \(A(1,2,3)\), \(B(2,-1,5)\) and \(C(-1,3,3)\).

  4. The plane which passes through \(A(1,2,3)\) and is parallel to the plane \(3x-5y+z=2\).

  5. The plane which passes through \(A(-2,1,5)\) and is perpendicular to the line of intersection of \(P_1: 3x+2y-z=5\) and \(P_2: -y+z=7\).

  6. The plane which contains the point \(A(-2,-1,3)\) and which contains the line \(L: x=1+t, y=3-2t, z=4t\).

  7. Consider the planes \(P_1: x+y+z=7\) and \(P_2: 2x+4z=6\).

    1. Compute an equation of the line of intersection of \(P_1\) and \(P_2\).

    2. Compute an equation of the plane which passes through the point \(A(1,2,3)\) and contains the line of intersection of \(P_1\) and \(P_2\).

  8. Find the acute angle of intersection of \(P_1: 3x-2y+5z=0\) and \(P_2: -x-y+2z=3\).

  9. Find the acute angle of intersection of \(P_1: 3x-2y-5z=0\) and \(P_2: -x-y+2z=3\).

  10. Consider the plane which passes through the point \(Q\) and whose normal vectors are parallel to \({\bf n}\). And, let \(P\) be another point in space, as illustrated below.

    1. Show that the distance between the point \(P\) and the given plane is \(d=\frac{|{\bf QP}\cdot{\bf n}|}{\|{\bf n}\|}\).

    2. Use this method to compute the distance between the point \(P(2,-1,4)\) and the plane \(x+2y+3z=5\).

  11. Consider planes \(P_1:2x-4y+5z=-2\) and \(P_2:x-2y+\frac{5}{2}z=5\).

    1. Verify that \(P_1\) and \(P_2\) are parallel.

    2. Compute the distance between \(P_1\) and \(P_2\). (Hint: See the previous problem.)
