Partial Fraction Decomposition

SUGGESTED REFERENCE MATERIAL:

As you work through the problems listed below, you should reference Chapter 7.5 of the recommended textbook (or the equivalent chapter in your alternative textbook/online resource) and your lecture notes.

EXPECTED SKILLS:

  • Be able to recognize an improper rational function, and perform the necessary long division to turn it into a proper rational function.

  • Know how to write down the partial fraction decomposition for a proper rational function, compute the unknown coefficients in the partial fractions, and integrate each partial fraction.

PRACTICE PROBLEMS:

For problems 18-19, evaluate the given integral by making substitution that transforms the problem into integrating a rational function.

  1. \(\int \frac{\sin{x}}{\cos^2{x}+6\cos{x}+5} \,dx\)



    \(\frac{1}{4}\ln{|\cos{x}+5|}-\frac{1}{4}\ln{|\cos{x}+1|}+C\)

  2. \(\int \frac{e^{5x}}{e^{4x}-1} \,dx\)



    \(e^{x}+\frac{1}{4}\ln|e^x-1|-\frac{1}{4}\ln(e^x+1)-\frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1}(e^x)+C\)

  3. By the end of this problem, you will know the antiderivatives of \(\sec{x}\). Observe the following:

    \[\begin{aligned} \int \sec{x} \,dx &= \int \frac{1}{\cos{x}} \,dx\\ &= \int \frac{\cos{x}}{\cos^2{x}} \,dx\\ &= \int \frac{\cos{x}}{1-\sin^2{x}} \,dx\end{aligned}\]

    1. Use the substitution \(u=\sin{x}\) to convert the given integral to an integral of a rational function.



      \(\int \frac{1}{1-u^2} \,du\)

    2. Use partial fractions to evaluate your integral from part (a). Show that the antiderivatives have the form \(\frac{1}{2}\ln{\left|\frac{u+1}{u-1}\right|}+C\)



      1 \[\begin{aligned} \int \frac{1}{1-u^2}\,du &= \int \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{u+1}\right)-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{u-1}\right) \,du\\ &=\frac{1}{2}\ln{|u+1|}-\frac{1}{2}\ln{|u-1|}+C\\ &=\frac{1}{2}\ln{\left|\frac{u+1}{u-1}\right|}+C\end{aligned}\]

    Notice that substituting back in for \(u\) yields:

    \[\begin{aligned} \int \sec{x} \,dx &= \frac{1}{2}\ln{\left|\frac{\sin{x}+1}{\sin{x}-1}\right|}+C\\ &=\frac{1}{2}\ln{\left|\frac{(\sin{x}+1)(\sin{x}+1)}{(\sin{x}-1)(\sin{x}+1)}\right|}+C\\ &=\frac{1}{2}\ln{\left|\frac{(\sin{x}+1)^2}{\sin^2{x}-1}\right|}+C\\ &=\frac{1}{2}\ln{\left|\frac{(\sin{x}+1)^2}{\cos^2{x}}\right|}+C\\ &=\ln{\left|\frac{\sin{x}+1}{\cos{x}}\right|}+C\\ &=\ln{\left|\tan{x}+\sec{x}\right|}+C\end{aligned}\]
