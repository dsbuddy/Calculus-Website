PRACTICE PROBLEMS:

For problems 1 and 2, compute the indicated derivative and state a corresponding integration formula.

  1. \(\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{1}{(2x+3)^2}\right]\)

    \(\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{1}{(2x+3)^2}\right]=\frac{-4}{(2x+3)^3} \implies \int \frac{-4}{(2x+3)^3} \,dx= \frac{1}{(2x+3)^2}+C\)

  2. \(\frac{d}{dx} [x\ln{x}-x]\)

    \(\frac{d}{dx} [x\ln{x}-x]=\ln{x} \implies \int \ln{x} \,dx = x\ln{x}-x+C\)

For problems 3-18, evaluate given indefinite integral and check your answer by differentiation.

  17. Consider \(\int{\cot^{2}{x}} \,dx\).

    1. Using the fact that \(\sin^{2}{x}+\cos^{2}{x}=1\), derive the identity \(\cot^2{x}=\csc^{2}{x}-1\).

      1 Provided that \(x \neq \pi \cdot k\), where \(k\) is any integer, we have: \[\begin{aligned} \sin^{2}{x}+\cos^{2}{x} &=1\\ \frac{\sin^{2}{x}}{\sin^{2}{x}}+\frac{\cos^{2}{x}}{\sin^2{x}}&=\frac{1}{\sin^2{x}}\\ 1+\cot^2{x}&=\csc^2{x}\\ \cot^2{x}&=\csc^2{x}-1\end{aligned}\]

    2. Use the identity that you derived in part (a) to evaluate the original integral.

      \(\int{\cot^{2}{x}} \,dx=-\cot{x}-x+C\).

For problems 20 and 21, find a function \(y=y(x)\) which satisfies the given Initial Value Problem.

  1. \(\left\{\begin{array}{l} \frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{1}{9x^2}\\ \\ y(1)=\frac{1}{2} \end{array}\right.\)

    \(y=-\frac{1}{9}x^{-1}+\frac{11}{18}\)

  2. \(\left\{\begin{array}{l} \frac{dy}{dx}=-2e^{x}\\ \\ y(0)=-5 \end{array}\right.\)

    \(y=-2e^{x}-3\)

  3. A ball is thrown straight up in the air from an initial height of \(s_0\) feet above the ground with an initial speed of \(v_0\) ft/sec. Then \(s(t)\) gives the height (in feet) above the ground at time \(t\), \(v(t)=s^{\prime}(t)\) gives the velocity (in ft/sec) of the ball at time \(t\), and \(a(t)=s^{\prime \prime}(t)\) gives the acceleration (in \(\text{ft/sec}^2\)) of the ball at time \(t\). Assuming that acceleration is constant, \(-g \text{ ft/sec}^2\), determine \(v(t)\) and \(s(t)\).

    \(v(t)=-gt+v_0\), \(s(t)=-\frac{1}{2}gt^2+v_0t+s_0\)
