  • Know how to use another part of the FTC to compute derivatives of functions defined as integrals.

  11. \(\int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}} \tan^2{x} \,dx\). (HINT: Use a trigonometric identity first to rewrite the integrand.)

  12. If an object moves along a straight line with position function \(s(t)\), its velocity function is \(v(t)=s^{\prime}(t)\). Then:

    • The from time \(t_1\) to time \(t_2\) is the net change of position of the particle during the time period from \(t_1\) to \(t_2\) and is calculated by evaluating\(\int_{t_1}^{t_2} v(t) \,dt\).

    • The from time \(t_1\) to time \(t_2\) is calculated by evaluating \(\int_{t_1}^{t_2} |v(t)| \,dt\).

    Assume that a particle is moving along a straight line such that its velocity at time \(t\) is \(v(t)=t^2-6t+5\) (meters per second).

    1. Compute the displacement of the particle during the time period \(0 \leq t \leq 6\).

    2. Compute the total distance traveled by the particle during the time period \(0 \leq t \leq 6\).

  13. The following Riemann Sum was derived by dividing an interval \([a,b]\) into \(n\) subintervals of equal width and then choosing \(x_k^*\) to be the right endpoint of each subinterval.

    \[\lim_{n \rightarrow +\infty} \sum_{k=1}^n{\left(1+\frac{4}{n}k\right)\frac{4}{n}}\]

    1. What is the interval, \([a,b]\)?

    2. Convert the Riemann Sum to an equivalent definite integral.

    3. Using the definite integral from part (b) and part of the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, evaluate the limit.

  14. Explain what is wrong with the following calculation:

    \[\int_{-1}^1 \frac{1}{x^2} \,dx =\left. -\frac{1}{x}\right|_{x=-1}^{x=1}=-1-(1)=-2\]

  6. Let \(F(x)=\int_1^x t\ln{t} \,dt\), for \(x>0\).

    2. Find all points \((x,y)\) where the graph of \(F(x)\) has a local (relative) maximum or a local (relative) minimum.

    3. Find the interval(s) on which \(F(x)\) is concave up and those on which \(F(x)\) is concave down.

    4. Determine the \(x\)-value(s) of each inflection point of \(F(x)\).
