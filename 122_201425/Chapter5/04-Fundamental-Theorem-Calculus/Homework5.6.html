  13. The following Riemann Sum was derived by dividing an interval \([a,b]\) into \(n\) subintervals of equal width and then choosing \(x_k^*\) to be the right endpoint of each subinterval.

    \[\lim_{n \rightarrow +\infty} \sum_{k=1}^n{\left(1+\frac{4}{n}k\right)\frac{4}{n}}\]

    1. What is the interval, \([a,b]\)?



      If we consider \(f(x)=x\), then the interval is \([1,5]\)

    2. Convert the Riemann Sum to an equivalent definite integral.



      \(\lim_{n \rightarrow +\infty} \sum_{k=1}^n{\left(1+\frac{4}{n}k\right)\frac{4}{n}}=\int_1^5{x} \,dx\)

    3. Using the definite integral from part (b) and part of the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, evaluate the limit.



      12
