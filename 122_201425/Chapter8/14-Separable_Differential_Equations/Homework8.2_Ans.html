  1. Verify that \(y=x^2+1\) is a solution to the differential equation \(y-\frac{dy}{dx}=(x-1)^2\).



    1Differentiating \(y=x^2+1\) with respect to \(x\) yields \(y^{\prime}=2x\). Thus,
    \[y-\frac{dy}{dx}=(x^2+1)-(2x)=(x-1)^2\]

  2. Find the value(s) of the constant \(A\) for which \(y=e^{Ax}\) is a solution to the differential equation \(y^{\prime\prime}+5y^{\prime}-6y=0\).



    \(A=-6\) and \(A=1\)

  5. Suppose that a ball is moving along a straight line through a resistive medium in such a way that its velocity \(v=v(t)\) decreases at a rate that is twice the square root of the velocity. Suppose that at time \(t=3\) seconds, the velocity of the ball is 16 meters/second.

      1Initial Value Problem: \(\left\{\begin{array}{l} \frac{dv}{dt}=-2\sqrt{v}\\ \\ v(3)=16 \end{array}\right.\); Solution: \(v(t)=(7-t)^2\)

For problems 16-18, use the results from problem 15.

  1. Carbon 14, an isotope of Carbon, is radioactive and decays at a rate proportional to the amount present (and therefore follows an exponential decay model). Its half life is 5730 years. If there were 20 grams of Carbon 14 originally present, how much would be left after 2000 years?



    \(y(t)=20(2)^{-t/5730}\). Thus, \(y(2000)=20(2)^{-2000/5730}\approx 15.702\) grams.

  2. A scientist wants to determine the half life of a certain radioactice substance. She determines that in exactly 5 days an 80 milligram sample decays to 10 milligrams. Based on this data, what is the half life?

  3. An unknown amount of a radioactive substance is being studied. After two days, the mass is 15 grams. After eight days, the mass is 9 grams. Assume exponential decay.
